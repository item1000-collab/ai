National Gifts Exhibition House in Pyongyang

A collection of diplomatic gifts at the foot of Mount Ryongak — June 2026

In June 2026, during a visit to Pyongyang, I had the opportunity to tour the National Gifts Exhibition House, located in the Mangyongdae District at the foot of Mount Ryongak. The complex is surrounded by dense forests and mountain slopes, giving it a distinct atmosphere of seclusion and solemnity.

The main entrance and front facade of the museum (taken by the author).

Architecture and exterior

The building is notable for its harmonious blend of traditional Korean elements — multi-tiered, curved green-tiled roofs and intricate woodwork — with a monumental stone foundation. A wide asphalt driveway and manicured landscaping with neatly trimmed spherical shrubs give the complex a respectful and stately appearance. The extensive scale of the structure against the backdrop of the mountains is truly impressive.

Massive carved wooden doors with golden ring handles at the main entrance.

The entrance is marked by massive carved wooden doors featuring geometric relief patterns and large golden ring handles, opening into a spacious vestibule finished in light marble.

Note on photography: Taking photographs inside the exhibition halls is strictly prohibited. The interior images shown in this article are sourced from the official DPRK album video created by professional guide.

History and scale of the collection

The museum was inaugurated in August 2012. According to official information, the collection comprises over 8,900 exhibits displayed across more than 20 rooms. The core of the collection consists of gifts presented to the country's leaders and members of their families. Donors include representatives of the Korean diaspora from around the world, as well as public organizations, political figures, and private individuals.

The museum's extensive complex with its signature green tiled roofs, nestled against the mountains.

Diversity of exhibits

The exposition covers a broad time span — from the 1970s to the present day. The halls feature a wide variety of decorative and applied arts, reflecting the cultural traditions of many countries.

The central hall with its distinctive white statues and softly illuminated backdrop (official album video).

In the central hall, two white full-length statues stand against a glowing pink panel — one of the most recognizable interiors of the museum. The space is designed with strict symmetry, featuring display cases along the sides and decorative vases.

A showcase featuring porcelain vases, a silver ship model, and a carved stone landscape (official album video).

The display cases contain a rich array of objects: delicate porcelain vases with portrait paintings, golden clocks with crystal pendants, silver models of ships, and massive sculptures made of semi-precious stones — such as a large white composition depicting snowy mountains and trees.

A finely carved white stone landscape with figures and pavilions (official album video).

Other halls showcase vibrant paintings, portraits, and landscape artworks, as well as various small decorative items — from miniature figurines to ornate cups and vases.

A general view of a display case with paintings, small statues, and ceremonial items (official album video).

A notable highlight is the automotive collection: a classic black limousine with chrome accents stands on a special platform. This is one of the rare exhibits that visitors are permitted to photograph.

A vintage black limousine with chrome detailing — one of the few items allowed for photography (taken by the author).

Among the most exquisite pieces is a three-panel folding screen featuring golden dragons embroidered on a dark green background, surrounded by waves and clouds — a fine example of meticulous handiwork.

A luxurious three-panel folding screen with golden dragon embroidery (official album video).

The collection also includes items reflecting international cultural ties, such as a brightly painted samovar in a Russian style and a large decorative horn with a silver rim.

A painted samovar and a ceremonial horn with silver mount (official album video).

Furniture hall and unique pieces

Special attention is drawn to a separate hall featuring elegant furniture sets: armchairs with gilded carvings and red leather upholstery, massive wardrobes, and refined tables.

Elegant furniture sets with gilded carvings and red leather upholstery (official album video).

The museum leaves an impression of a rich and diverse collection, where fine jewelry art, delicate handcraft, traditional folk crafts, and prestigious gifts from foreign delegations coexist. The exhibition vividly reflects international relations and cultural exchange recorded in material objects.
