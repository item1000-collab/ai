Buddhism & Juche: two philosophies that inspire North Korea

How a country with an atheist ideology carefully preserves Buddhist monasteries and builds its future on principles of self-reliance.

When we think of North Korea, stereotypes often come to mind. But the reality we saw during our visit to the DPRK was completely different. It is a country that cherishes its past, restores ancient temples, and follows a philosophy that teaches self-reliance.

Today we will talk about two surprising phenomena that coexist peacefully in this country: Buddhism and the Juche idea. There is much to marvel at.

Buddhism in North Korea: preserving an ancient tradition

Many are surprised to learn that there are Buddhist monasteries in the DPRK. After all, the country is officially considered atheist. However, in practice, the state not only does not ban Buddhism but also supports it as part of the cultural heritage.

We visited several monasteries. The monks welcomed us warmly, showed us around, and told us about the history. Most importantly, we saw that the temples are in excellent condition. The state allocates funds for their restoration. It was unexpected but very pleasant.

Buddhism arrived on the Korean Peninsula in the 4th century. Over 1,500 years, it became an integral part of national identity. Even in modern North Korea, this heritage is treated with great respect.

Why Buddhism endures

  • It is part of the country’s thousand-year history.
  • Buddhist monasteries are architectural and cultural monuments.
  • The state sees their value for future generations.

North Korean Buddhists do not oppose the state. They simply continue their tradition — praying, teaching, preserving ancient knowledge. And they are allowed to do so.

Juche — a philosophy of creation and self-reliance

What is Juche?

Translated from Korean — "self-reliance". This philosophy became the foundation for the country’s development. But if we remove the political context, the essence of Juche is simple and inspiring:

  • A person can achieve everything on their own.
  • The country must develop with its own hands.
  • Labour is the basis of prosperity.

For the people of the DPRK, Juche is not just a theory. It is a way of life. They believe that only diligent work and belief in their own strength can make the country strong and independent.

“They create everything themselves — electric buses, the metro, buildings — everything is built without help from other countries. It’s impressive. They really believe they can rely only on themselves.”

— a traveller’s reflection

In Pyongyang, there is the Monument to the Juche Idea — a majestic tower 170 metres high. It symbolises aspiration to the future, faith in one’s people and one’s strength. Tourists are taken to the top so they can see the city from above and immerse themselves in this atmosphere of creation.

Harmony of opposites

At first glance, Buddhism and Juche seem like opposites. Buddhism teaches contemplation and inner peace. Juche — active labour and development.

But in reality they have common traits:

🧘 Buddhism

  • Respect for traditions
  • Inner discipline
  • Self-reliance on the spiritual path
  • Harmony with the surrounding world

⚒️ Juche

  • Respect for the past
  • Labour discipline
  • Self-reliance in life
  • Harmony through creation

These systems do not conflict. They work on different levels:

  • Buddhism cares for the soul and preserves the cultural code.
  • Juche inspires development and building the future.

“It seemed like a strange combination, but we saw that it works. People work, the country develops, and ancient traditions are preserved. Everything is very positive.”

— a tourist’s impression

Everything we saw was positive. Cleanliness, order, respect for culture, diligence. Yes, it is a different country. But there is something inspiring about it — their faith in themselves and their strength.

Buddhism and Juche — two pillars on which North Korean culture rests. One is the wisdom of antiquity. The second is the philosophy of the future. Together they create a unique world where tradition and development go hand in hand.
