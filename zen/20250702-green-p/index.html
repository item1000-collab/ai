🇰🇵 Pyongyang · first day

Green Pyongyang, Triumphal Arch & the 38th‑floor view

✈️ AI Travel & Language Lab · 15 June 2026

The plane landed in Pyongyang around two in the afternoon. Stepping out of the airport, I didn’t immediately realise I was in North Korea — everything felt… ordinary. But within an hour I understood: this place is a living contrast to what the news shows.

First impression: impeccable cleanliness

The first thing that struck me was absolute cleanliness. Not a speck of dust, not a scrap of paper on the streets. The city looks thoroughly modern: wide avenues, high‑rises, well‑tended lawns.

⭐ Fact that shook me:

During the Korean War, American aviation literally erased Pyongyang from the map. Only the East and West Gates survived from the old city. Everything else was rebuilt from scratch.

And Pyongyang turned out to be very green. Trees line the streets — ginkgo with fan‑shaped leaves, conifers, apricots. Lawns are cared for like park flowerbeds, tree trunks regularly whitewashed. This is not a “grim” country — it is a well‑kept, almost European city.

🌿 Green & groomed

Ginkgo, pine, apricot trees — and every trunk whitewashed with care. The parks are immaculate.

From the airport straight to the tour

First stop — the Triumphal Arch. We climbed to the top; from there the view opened onto the square and the stadium. The scale is impressive: it’s not just an arch, but a whole memorial complex.

🏛️ Triumphal Arch

Built to commemorate Korea’s liberation from Japanese colonialism.

🎏 Main square

Spacious, ceremonial — venue for all festive parades.

Then we walked onto Pyongyang’s main square — vast, formal, the place for all ceremonial processions. There I saw local residents for the first time: women in light dresses, men in navy trousers and shirts. Neat and restrained.

👥 Local style

Women in light dresses, men in dark‑blue trousers and shirts. Accurate & modest

Hotel Yanggakdo: a view over the whole city

We checked into the Yanggakdo Hotel on the 38th floor. It stands on the island of the same name in the middle of the Taedong River. From the windows — a breathtaking view of Pyongyang: the lights of high‑rises, the river bend, mountain silhouettes on the horizon.

🔍 Observation:

Not only foreigners stayed at the hotel, but also Koreans themselves. That was the first signal that life here is not as “closed” as is commonly thought.

✨ Main impression of the first day

On the first day I understood the main thing: everything I had known about the DPRK before the trip turned out to be only the tip of the iceberg. Pyongyang is beautiful, clean and green. People are friendly and smiling, they walk in the evenings with their families.

This is not the country from the news. It’s ordinary life — you just have to see it with your own eyes.
