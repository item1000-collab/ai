🎲 GAME ONE · VOCABULARY QUICKFIRE (20 questions style)
Miles: Welcome everyone to the one and only The Word War. The show where grammar is the weapon and vocabulary is the victory. I'm your host Miles. In one corner, Anatoly. In the other, Peter. 20 questions, points on the line.
Miles: Question one: What's the word for when two things happen at the exact same time by pure chance? Spooky kind of luck.
Anatoly: Coincidence?
Miles: Boom, coincidence is exactly right. Point to whoever spoke first — Peter takes the lead, 1–0.
Miles: Question two: Someone who is extremely stubborn, refusing to change their mind no matter what. Starts with O.
Peter: Obstinate?
Miles: Exactly — obstinate. Means totally refusing to budge. No points for anyone this round.
Miles: Question three: Something very very old, from a thousand years ago or more.
Miles: (slips) Ancient! That's an automatic point.
Peter: I said ancient. Another point for me.
Miles: Total showmaster fail. Peter 2–0.
Miles: Question six: Someone very skilled or experienced in a particular field, a total pro. Starts with E.
Anatoly: Erudite?
Peter: Expert.
Miles: Expert is the word. Point to Peter — now 3–2.
Miles: Question seven: Completely and utterly exhausted, like after a marathon. Can't move a finger.
Anatoly: Exhausted — but you said it already. Completely spent?
Miles: I'm a disaster. Point to Peter. 4–2.
Miles: Question eight: Very cautious, fearful of taking risks, won't leave comfort zone.
Peter & Anatoly: Conservative.
Miles: Conservative works. Draw — score still 4–2.
Miles: Question nine: Something completely and utterly fake, a total sham, fabrication.
Anatoly: Scam!
Miles: Peter swoops in last second and steals the point — 5–2. Peter takes the trophy, the legendary Golden Dictionary, and total bragging rights.
🎭 GAME TWO · CONTEXT CLASH + BLANKETY-BLANK
Miles: New game: Context Clash. I give you a versatile word and two different scenarios. You compete to fit the word into a sentence that fits the vibe. Literal vs figurative.
Miles: Example: word 'cold'. Scenario one: fancy ballroom → "the duchess gave a cold stare." Scenario two: rainy street → "the case was cold and the rain colder." Same word, different energy.
Anatoly: Understood — first is emotional, second is temperature.
Miles: Exactly. Here's your word: sharp. Scenario one: elite corporate boardroom. Scenario two: chaotic family kitchen during dinner rush.
Anatoly: Sharp negotiation in boardroom, sharp knife in kitchen.
Miles: You've got it. Next word: bright. Sunny beach / smartest student in class.
Peter: Bright sunlight at the beach, and a bright student — very intelligent.
Miles: Flawless. Word: cool. Winter mountain / someone who stays calm when everything goes wrong.
Anatoly: Cool mountain breeze and a cool-headed person.
Miles: Perfect. Word: light. Dark room with a single candle / person who doesn't take life too seriously, loves to joke.
Peter: A flicker of light in darkness, and a lighthearted joker.
Miles: Nailed it. You've mastered the literal vs figurative shift.
Miles: Now Blankety-Blank: I say a sentence, leave out one word and say "blank". You guess the missing word.
Miles: "The movie was so BLANK that I actually fell asleep halfway through."
Peter: Boring.
Miles: Boom, right on the money. Point to Peter.
Miles: "The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely BLANK."
Anatoly: Amazing.
Miles: Ding ding ding — Anatoly strikes back.
Miles: "I tried to bake a cake, but I forgot the BLANK, so it tasted like cardboard."
Peter: Sugar!
Miles: That's the one. Peter dominates again. You've mastered vocabulary, inference and context.
Miles: That's all for today. Next show tomorrow — redemption arc. Anatoly is coming for that trophy.
