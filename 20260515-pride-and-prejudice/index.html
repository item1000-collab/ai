📖 Key vocabulary & expressions
  • opulence
    noun — great wealth, luxury, and richness; abundance of valuable possessions.
    📽️ "Netherfield mansion's opulence contrasts with the muddy reality of Elizabeth's walk."
  • linger
    verb — to stay in a place or position for a long time, often longer than necessary.
    🎥 "The camera refuses to linger on static period spectacle — it keeps moving."
  • aesthetic
    noun / adj — a set of principles concerned with beauty or artistic taste.
    🌾 "There is an aesthetic of poverty: muddy hems look beautiful and authentic in the 2005 film."
  • dialectical clash
    phrase — a direct opposition of two ideas or worldviews, often leading to deeper understanding.
    ⚖️ "Dialectical clash of cinematic ideologies: ideal Hollywood vs raw realism."
  • gilded frame / muddy hem
    metaphor — gilded = covered with gold (artificial perfection); muddy hem = real life, dirt, hardship.
    🖼️ "The left slide shows a gilded frame; the right — muddy hem of progress."
  • static pictorialism
    cinematography term — camera style that remains still, creating painting-like, composed images.
    📸 "1940 adaptation used static pictorialism, while 2005 uses kinetic camera circling Elizabeth."
🎭 Austen & adaptations · cultural lens
📜 Jane Austen's voice + autobiography
Anatoly notes that Austen likely drew from her own life — falling in love with a wealthy man whose family disapproved due to her lower wealth. The novel mirrors that social friction. Class and irony become central.
⚖️ 1940 MGM: Studio System & 'eradication of conflict'
Golden Age Hollywood elegance → puffed sleeves, massive bonnets, visual comedy. The romance is polished, static characters: Elizabeth is "sweet, spirited but fundamentally static". Entertainment over gritty social tension.
🌧️ 2005: muddy hem & social realism
Director Joe Wright embraces agrarian realism: Elizabeth walks miles, gets muddy, fiercely independent (tomboyish). The camera is kinetic, circling her. Historical props feel 3D. Class contrast shown via real locations: Netherfield opulence vs humble Longbourn.
📽️ cinematography as ideology
"Camera refuses to linger" — instead of static period spectacle, the 2005 film uses kinetic cinematography to render emotions and social mobility. Riley highlights: "The mechanical motion renders historical props three-dimensional."
