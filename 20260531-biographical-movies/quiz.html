🎞️ Real Lives on Screen

Biographical Movies | English through Stories
🎭 What is a biographical film?
A biopic tells the true story of a real person’s life. Today we explore four powerful biographies about musicians, royals, spies, and legends. Watch, read, and test your understanding!
👉 Class focus: listen for key events, check comprehension, discuss real-life themes.
💬 Think about it: Why do you think directors make movies about real people?
🤔 Possible answer (click)
✔️ To inspire audiences, explore history, understand famous personalities, and bring important true stories to life.

📅 2010 | 🎭 Biography / Drama
👑 The King's Speech
📖 Short summary: Prince Albert (later King George VI) unexpectedly becomes king of England. He suffers from a severe stammer (speech difficulty). With the help of an unorthodox speech therapist, Lionel Logue, he finds his voice and leads his country through World War II. A touching story of courage, friendship, and determination.
📝 Comprehension check (True / False)
1️⃣ King George VI was born to be king from the beginning. ❓ Reveal answer
❌ False — He became king after his brother abdicated (gave up the throne).
2️⃣ The speech therapist Lionel Logue used unusual methods to help the king. ❓ Reveal answer
✅ True — He made him sing, curse, and do physical exercises.
✏️ Short answer: Name one challenge King George VI had to overcome.
Show sample answer
His stammer / fear of public speaking / lack of confidence.
⭐ Theme: Perseverance & the power of unexpected friendship
📅 2009 | 🎸 Music / Biography
🎵 Nowhere Boy
📖 Short summary: A portrait of a young John Lennon before The Beatles became famous. Growing up in 1950s Liverpool, John is torn between two women: his strict aunt Mimi, who raised him, and his free-spirited mother, Julia, who re-enters his life. The film shows how teenage anger, love, and rock ‘n’ roll shape his artistic destiny.
✔️ True or False?
1️⃣ John Lennon lived with his mother Julia for his whole childhood. ❓ Answer
❌ False — He was raised mainly by his aunt Mimi.
2️⃣ The film shows the beginning of John’s interest in music. ❓ Answer
✅ True — He discovers rock and roll and forms his first band.
🎤 Short answer: What city does the movie take place in?
Reveal
Liverpool, England.
🎸 Theme: Family relationships & finding your creative identity
📅 2005 | 🎤 Musical Biography / Romance
🎸 Walk the Line
📖 Short summary: The life story of country music legend Johnny Cash, from his poor cotton-farming childhood in Arkansas to his rise at Sun Records in Memphis. The film focuses on his struggles with addiction, his love for June Carter, and his legendary Folsom Prison concert. A powerful story of redemption.
🔍 True / False
1️⃣ Johnny Cash recorded music together with Elvis Presley and Jerry Lee Lewis at Sun Records. ❓ Check
✅ True — They were all part of the “Million Dollar Quartet” era.
2️⃣ June Carter helped Johnny Cash overcome drug problems. ❓ Check
✅ True — She supported him to get clean.
🎶 Short answer: Where does Johnny Cash perform a famous live album? (Hint: prison)
Answer
Folsom Prison (California).
🔥 Theme: Redemption, addiction, and love as a saving grace
📅 2018 | 🕵️ Spy Drama / Biography
🕊️ Red Joan
📖 Short summary: Based on the true story of Melita Norwood (called Joan in the film), a British woman who secretly passed nuclear secrets to the Soviet Union during the Cold War. Decades later, an elderly Joan is arrested by MI5. The movie explores her motivations: idealism, fear of nuclear monopoly, and loyalty to friends.
🧠 True / False
1️⃣ “Red Joan” is a completely fictional character, not a real spy. ❓ Answer
❌ False — It’s inspired by the real-life spy Melita Norwood.
2️⃣ Joan shared secrets because she wanted money and luxury. ❓ Answer
❌ False — She did it for ideological reasons (preventing one country from having all nuclear power).
🌍 Short answer: Which war/cold conflict provides the background of this film?
Show
The Cold War (nuclear arms race).
⚖️ Theme: Morality, loyalty, and difficult political choices

Talk & Reflect

🗣️ Group discussion / writing prompts (B1–B2)

• Which of these real-life figures do you admire the most? Why?
• “The King’s Speech” shows a leader overcoming a personal weakness. What can we learn from that?
• “Nowhere Boy” and “Walk the Line” focus on musicians’ early struggles. Why is it important to see their hardships?
• In “Red Joan”, the main character commits espionage for what she believed was right. Is it ever OK to break the law for your ideals?
• Imagine you could interview one person from these movies. What would you ask?
