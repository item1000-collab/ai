🇬🇪 Sakartvelo: soul and stone

Lesson based on a conversation between Anatoly & Jilda · 07 July 2026

🏔️ Thematic blocks history · architecture · regions

Ancient roots & Christianity: Dmanisi skulls (1.8 million years), Colchis & the Golden Fleece, adoption of Christianity in 337 AD – Saint Nino’s cross at Jvari.

Tbilisi & urban fabric: founded on sulfur springs (5th c.), Narikala Fortress, Abanotubani baths, Rustaveli Avenue, Metekhi Church, carved wooden balconies and “Italian yards”.

Mountain watchtowers & regions: Svaneti towers (Koshki), Ushguli – Europe’s highest inhabited settlement, Kazbegi (Stepantsminda) & Gergeti Trinity Church, remote Tusheti and Khevsureti.

Wine & supra: Georgia as the cradle of wine, the tradition of the supra (feast) – a synthesis of Georgian soul.

💬 Lesson dialogue (excerpts)

Anatoly:I created a presentation with AI – it’s about your country, Georgia. In Georgian it’s Sakartvelo.
Jilda:Exactly. Sakartvelo. You are correct. The Dmanisi skulls are some of the oldest discovered outside Africa. Their names were Zezva and Mzia.
Anatoly:I visited Georgia as a child – we stayed in Kobuleti near Batumi and visited Tbilisi for one day.
Jilda:Narikala Fortress overlooks the whole city. Abanotubani – historic sulfur baths – people still go there for medical purposes or just to enjoy.
Jilda:The carved wooden balconies are quintessential Georgian façades. We call them “Italian yards” – shared courtyards with grapevine trellises.
Anatoly:And the mountain watchtowers – Svaneti, Kazbegi, Tusheti…
Jilda:Yes, Svaneti towers are up to five stories high, walls thick enough to survive seismic activity and heavy snow. Ushguli is Europe’s highest permanent settlement.
Anatoly:We also talked about movies – I shared my list. You’ve seen The Terminal, The King’s Speech, Dead Poets Society…
Jilda:Yes, I’ve seen many. I don’t really like AI-generated content, but this presentation gives a brief, correct picture of Georgia. It’s just fine.

📚 Advanced vocabulary & idioms

  • quintessential adj. representing the most perfect or typical example of a quality or class.
  • communal courtyard phrase a shared open space used by multiple families, often with grapevines or roses.
  • peeling wallpaper / hidden opulence concealed wealth (Art Nouveau, stained glass) beneath later Soviet-era additions.
  • seismic activity earthquake-related movement; the towers were built to withstand centuries of tremors.
  • ultra‑remote extremely far from populated areas; difficult to access (Tusheti, Khevsureti).
  • supra Georgian traditional feast, a synthesis of hospitality, wine, and polyphonic singing.
