🌍 Countries · culture & perspective

Target vocabulary · thematic blocks · discussion

🏛️ 1. Governance & infrastructure vocab

  • Constitutional monarchy – a system where a monarch acts as head of state within constitutional guidelines.
  • Federal republic – a federation of states with a central government and shared sovereignty.
  • Public welfare – government support for citizens' well-being (healthcare, education, etc.).
Parliamentary · system Devolution · power transfer

🧳 2. Cultural identity & heritage idioms

  • Melting pot – a place where different cultures blend and coexist.
  • Crossroads of civilizations – a geographic or cultural area where diverse influences meet.
  • Cultural tapestry – the intricate and varied pattern of a society's traditions.
Heritage · legacy Folkloric · traditional

📊 3. Economy & development key terms

  • Emerging market – an economy in the process of rapid growth and industrialization.
  • GDP per capita – gross domestic product divided by population; a measure of average wealth.
  • Trade surplus – when exports exceed imports, boosting national income.
Inflation · pricing Purchasing power · value

In a nutshell, today's lesson with Simona and Anatoly revolved around the multifaceted nature of countries — from governance models to cultural identity. We explored how a country's constitutional framework shapes daily life, and how cultural tapestry enriches the experience of both locals and expats. Anatoly showed a keen interest in the intricate balance between economic development and social welfare, which often defines a nation's standing on the global stage.

Beyond the technical terms, we highlighted idiomatic expressions that bring colour to discussions about nations: “melting pot” (diverse cultures), “crossroads of civilizations” (geographic convergence), “to go the extra mile” (making additional effort to understand a new culture), “to break the ice” (initiate communication across cultural gaps), and “to think outside the box” (approach country comparisons creatively). These phrases, paired with precise vocabulary, empower learners to express nuanced opinions about any country they encounter.
