🏛️ Thematic blocks Jordan
📜 Ancient crossroads
  • Petra (The Rose Red City) – Nabataean rock‑cut city, carved directly into rock faces without modern machinery. Built by Nabataeans (ancient nomadic traders) around 1st Century BC.
  • Al Khazneh (The Treasury) – shows blend of Greek and Nabataean artistic styles.
  • Jerash (Pompeii of the East) – one of the best preserved Roman cities; paved streets, temples, fountains.
  • Wadi Rum – desert valley, film location for Lawrence of Arabia, The Martian, Dune, Star Wars, and Transformers [citation:13][citation:14].
  • Nabataeans – masters of water engineering: ceramic pipes, dams, oasis creation.
🌿 Nature & ecology
  • Dead Sea – lowest point on Earth (≈430 m below sea level), mineral‑rich, hypersaline (≈10× saltier than ocean), no fish, therapeutic use, effortless floating [citation:9][citation:11].
  • Azraq wetlands – “blue jewel” oasis for migrating birds.
  • Debim Forest – steep hills covered in ancient Aleppo pines.
  • Amman – capital, “authentic and vintage”, built across hills.
  • Hashemite Kingdom – legendary hospitality, kahwa (Arabic coffee).
🔍 Interesting facts from the web
🏗️ Petra's hidden water engineering

Recent research (2023) uncovered that Petra's 'Ain Braq aqueduct was far more complex than previously thought — it featured two distinct conduits in successive phases: a pressurized lead-pipeline and a system of open channels with terracotta pipes [citation:1]. The Nabataeans supported a population of 30,000–40,000 people in the desert using sophisticated water systems [citation:4].

🏛️ Jerash — more than just Roman ruins

The site of Jerash has evidence of settlement dating back to 7500 BC (Neolithic period) [citation:6][citation:7]. The city was known as Gerasa and nicknamed "Pompeii of the East" or "City of 1000 Columns" [citation:5][citation:6]. Emperor Hadrian visited in AD 129–130, and the Arch of Hadrian was built to commemorate his visit [citation:5].

🧂 Dead Sea — unique salt giants

The Dead Sea is the second-saltiest lake in the world with a salt concentration above 30% [citation:11]. It is the only place where scientists can study the formation of "salt giants" — massive salt deposits up to 1 km thick — in real time through a process called double-diffusive convection [citation:12]. The lake level has been dropping at about 1.2 meters per year due to water diversion from the Jordan River [citation:11].

🎬 Wadi Rum — Hollywood's favourite otherworldly location

Wadi Rum has been used for over 600 film projects annually [citation:14]. It has doubled for Mars in The Martian and Red Planet, and served as a backdrop for Star Wars, Dune, and Lawrence of Arabia [citation:13][citation:14]. The 2014 Jordanian film Theeb, shot entirely in Wadi Rum, was nominated for an Oscar [citation:13].
