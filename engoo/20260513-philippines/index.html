🔍 Key expressions & contextual usage
pristine (adj.) laid‑back (adj.) imbue (verb) sleeper hit (n.) off the beaten path (idiom) windswept vibrant tapestry (metaphor) colonial fusion

During the presentation, Jen explains that "laid‑back" means no rush, a relaxed environment; "sleeper hit" refers to something that becomes unexpectedly popular (like Siargao for surfing); "off the beaten path" describes remote, non‑touristy locations (Banaue rice terraces). These expressions are essential for travel storytelling and advanced English exams (IELTS/TOEFL).

📖 English deep dive
Key terms from "Philippine Field Notes" with contextual examples.
🌿 pristine /ˈprɪstiːn/
original, pure, untouched. "The lagoons of Palawan have pristine waters."
🧘 laid‑back (adj.)
relaxed, unhurried. "Boracay offers a laid‑back island vibe."
✨ imbue (with)
to fill or inspire thoroughly. "Filipino festivals are imbued with community spirit."
🏄 sleeper hit
unexpected success. "Siargao was a sleeper hit among international surfers."
🗺️ off the beaten path
remote, not frequented by tourists. "Banaue's rice terraces are off the beaten path."
🎭 vibrant tapestry
metaphor for rich, multi‑layered culture. "Philippine history is a vibrant tapestry of indigenous and colonial threads."
🌬️ windswept
exposed to strong winds. "The hills of Batanes are windswept and atmospheric."
🧠 Grammar spotlight: using adjectives to create imagery. Instead of "nice beach" → "pristine shoreline with a laid‑back energy."
