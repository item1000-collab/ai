🇰🇵 North Korea: impressions & culture

Lesson based on a travel dialogue · July 2026

📌 Thematic blocks travel · society · daily life

  • First impressions: Pyongyang’s architecture, clean streets, modern buildings.
  • Everyday life: cars, clothes, children’s education, and the role of the state.
  • Social norms: marriage age (24–27), rare divorces, government flats for newlyweds.
  • Attitude toward leaders: deep respect, portraits everywhere, “comrade” titles, badges.
  • Religion & heritage: Buddhist monasteries, historical sites preserved.
  • Economy & souvenirs: ginseng tea, forest berries, high‑quality backpacks, broderie (embroidery).
🎯 Target vocabulary: prosperous, trajectory, guidance, sincere, exclusive, regulated, broderie

💬 Excerpts from the conversation

ElhamWhat is the difference between South and North Korea?
AnatolyI can’t say because I only visited North Korea. But from what we know, it is a prosperous country with good economics and beautiful, clean streets.
ElhamWhy did you write “souvenir” here?
AnatolyWe bought instant tea with ginseng and forest berries. We also visited a backpack factory — excellent quality, durable and comfortable.
ElhamWhat about marriage and divorce?
AnatolyGirls marry on average at 24–27. Divorce is very rare, seen as unusual. The guide said she got a free flat from the government after her marriage.
ElhamIs the respect toward leaders sincere?
AnatolyFor us it looked strange, but we accepted it. They wear badges with portraits, and leaders are seen almost like gods. It’s part of their education from childhood.
ElhamDid you buy anything?
AnatolyYes, two embroideries (broderie). One is quite big, with traditional clothes and flower symbols. Very detailed.
ElhamWhat was the strangest thing?
AnatolyOnly the attitude toward the leaders. But we respected their feelings — they were sincere, I think.

* Greetings, technical issues, and farewells have been removed for clarity.

📝 Summary

First impressions & society. Anatoly describes his visit to North Korea with a tour group. He highlights Pyongyang’s clean, modern streets, the absence of historical buildings due to war, and the well‑kept countryside. He notes that North Koreans dress well, cars are common (contrary to Western media), and the state provides housing after marriage. The society is highly traditional: early marriage is expected, divorce is rare, and family is central.

Culture, leadership & souvenirs. The most striking aspect is the reverence for leaders — portraits everywhere, badges worn by all, and a quasi‑religious respect. Anatoly and his group accepted this as genuine. He also bought embroidered artworks (broderie) as souvenirs. The lesson contrasts North Korea with Iran, touching on security, nuclear capabilities, and the wish for peace. Despite the political tension, Anatoly expresses interest in visiting Iran someday.

✦ Key takeaway: The dialogue offers a rare, first‑hand glimpse into everyday life in North Korea — beyond propaganda — while exploring cultural values, leadership, and the personal experience of a foreign traveller.
