🏝️ Thematic Block: Discovering Cuba — Varadero & Vintage Charm

In 2024, Anatoly traveled to Cuba and explored the stunning Varadero peninsula — a destination reserved for tourists with pristine beaches and crystal-clear Atlantic waters. He documented his journey through photographs: from the iconic “gate to Varadero” toll booth to the turquoise ocean and local wildlife like pelicans. One of the most memorable sights was the endless parade of vintage American cars from the 1950s, preserved and still driving on Cuban streets. Anatoly described the feeling as “being in another epoch.”

🌟 Travel vocabulary in context: “peninsula,” “reserved for tourists,” “retro cars,” “crystal waters,” “everyday life from the bus” — language that helps learners narrate past journeys with vivid details.

📖 Travel & Descriptive Vocabulary
  • 🔹 peninsula – a piece of land almost surrounded by water or projecting out into a body of water. “Varadero is a peninsula reserved for tourists.”
  • 🔹 vintage / retro cars – classic, old-fashioned vehicles from an earlier era, often restored. “Lots of retro cars appeared — it felt like being in another epoch.”
  • 🔹 crystal waters – extremely clear, transparent water, often used to describe tropical beaches. “The ocean has such beautiful color, crystal waters.”
  • 🔹 anticipation of future traveling – the feeling of excitement and expectation before a journey. “This picture shows anticipation of future traveling.”
  • 🔹 everyday life (from the bus) – ordinary, typical scenes of local daily activities, often observed while traveling. “Everyday life from the bus — white rubber boots.”
➕ Bonus idiom: “feeling like being in another epoch” — a strong expression meaning experiencing a completely different time period.
🤖 AI-Assisted Travel Presentations

Anatoly created a virtual trip presentation using artificial intelligence. He uploaded his original photos, and the AI generated cartoon-style recreations with vocabulary labels (e.g., “lagoon,” “open shore,” “inland water”). This tool, called Notebook LM, helps English learners connect images with descriptive words. The AI also produced a mood-matching quiz where students guess feelings like “nostalgia,” “solitude,” “rock beauty,” or “hospitality” — turning travel memories into interactive language practice.

Example from the quiz: A photo of an empty beach with a single boat — the correct mood is “meditation,” not just “serenity,” because the image evokes active mental stillness.
