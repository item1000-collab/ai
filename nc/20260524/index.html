Reading 1: Trump’s Iran Deal – Diplomacy Over Escalation

Source: Responsible Statecraft • May 23, 2026 • By Trita Parsi

Trita Parsi delivers a sharp, optimistic analysis of a potential breakthrough in U.S.-Iran relations following the recent conflict. According to the piece, President Trump has signaled a significant agreement that would restore a regional ceasefire, ease some sanctions, and open a 30-day window for a more comprehensive nuclear and maritime deal.

“In essence, this agreement brings the situation to where it was supposed to be following the announcement of the original ceasefire.”

Key Strengths of the Article:

  • Excellent contextualization of the deal’s regional implications
  • Clear-eyed assessment of spoilers (especially Israel and Washington hawks)
  • Insightful commentary on Europe’s diminishing role in Middle East diplomacy
  • Balanced recognition of domestic political challenges for both Trump and Iranian leadership

The piece convincingly argues that broad regional support (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, etc.) gives Trump political cover that Obama lacked in 2015. However, Parsi rightly warns that the next 30 days are high-risk due to potential sabotage efforts.

Reading 2: Xi Jinping’s Contrasting Summits with Trump and Putin

Source: The Korea Times • May 22, 2026

This report offers a fascinating comparative look at China’s strategic diplomacy. While Xi rolled out the red carpet for Trump with ceremonial pomp and symbolic gestures (including a rare tour of Zhongnanhai), the meeting with Putin was substantive, resulting in over 40 cooperation agreements and a strong reaffirmation of their “no limits” partnership.

Key Observations:

  • Stabilization vs. Deepening: China is trying to manage tensions with the U.S. while solidifying its axis with Russia.
  • Taiwan Divergence: Russia offers unconditional support for China’s position; Trump treats arms sales as a negotiating chip.
  • Energy & Multipolarity: Russia-China ties are framed in terms of energy security and a shared vision of a multipolar world.

The contrast is telling: ceremonial respect for Trump, concrete deliverables for Putin. Notably, no major new gas pipeline deal was announced with Russia, which the article suggests is a setback for Moscow.

Overall Review: 8.5/10 – Highly Insightful Pairing

These two pieces together paint a compelling picture of the current global moment:

  • A U.S. administration attempting pragmatic deal-making in the Middle East while navigating intense domestic and Israeli opposition.
  • China skillfully playing both sides — stabilizing relations with Washington while tightening strategic bonds with Moscow.

The bigger picture emerging: The post-conflict landscape is one of exhausted escalation. Trump appears to be pivoting toward transactional diplomacy, while China continues its patient, dual-track strategy of economic engagement and authoritarian alignment.

Both articles are well-reported and avoid cheap sensationalism, though Parsi’s piece carries a clearer editorial perspective. Together, they offer essential reading for anyone trying to understand the rapidly evolving 2026 geopolitical environment.

Strongly recommended for students of international relations, foreign policy analysts, and anyone tracking great power competition.
