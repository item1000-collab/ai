📰 “PRISM, Snowden, and Surveillance”
Virtlantis SIM in Second Life (SL)

📅 Saturday, June 15, 2013 ⏱️ 7:00 – 8:00 AM SLT 📍 Sandy Bend @ VIF, Virtlantis 👤 Organizer: Shiaida Paliranta
🖼️ Event screenshot (original SL image): 📸 2013 06 15 SL.jpg Second Life · Virtlantis · June 15, 2013

🗣️ What happened?

Just ten days after Edward Snowden’s first revelations about the NSA’s PRISM program became public, the Virtlantis community convened in-world for a politically charged discussion. The setting, Sandy Bend, reflected the surreal and exploratory atmosphere often associated with Virtlantis, while the subject matter focused on real-world surveillance, privacy, and government oversight.

💬 The discussion (as seen in the image)

Shiaida Paliranta structured the session around four discussion prompts:

1. “Is Snowden a traitor or a hero?”
Opinions appeared divided. Some participants viewed Snowden as a whistleblower defending civil liberties, while others focused on the risks associated with leaking classified material.

2. “If you are innocent, you have nothing to worry about.”
Most attendees challenged this statement, arguing that mass surveillance can threaten privacy rights regardless of innocence.

3. “Discuss anything that caught your attention in the articles.”
Conversation centered on topics such as FISA courts, metadata collection, and alleged direct access to major technology platforms.

4. “Ask the group a question beginning with ‘Why’ or ‘How.’”
Questions explored trust in oversight committees, intelligence accountability, and international information-sharing practices.

⭐ Final verdict

A fascinating time capsule. The event demonstrated how Second Life could function as an alternative public discussion space for major global events. Rather than a formal lecture, the session felt like an open, spontaneous exchange between participants engaging with urgent political and ethical questions.

Would attend again? Yes — though a longer session format would allow for deeper discussion.
