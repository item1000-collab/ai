❶ The gathering storm · Europe

⚔️ Deterrence paradox & societal resilience slide 2

European leaders increase defence budgets not to fight in Ukraine, but because a kinetic war beyond Ukraine seems plausible. Yet a 2024 Gallup poll shows ~33% of Europeans would refuse to fight if their own country were attacked – Italy at the bottom (78% unwilling). slide 3

The Fog of Peace prevents cognitive preparation for survival. slide 5

🗺️ Geography dictates plausibility: outer breakwaters (Estonia) vs sheltered harbors (Italy). slide 4

🛡️ Ukrainian resolve vs European fear slide 2, 6

A Ukrainian official: “We’re not superhuman, we’re just at war.” War transforms cognition and unleashes creativity. Europeans abhor war, but without war, preparedness fades – weakening deterrence.

“Can the spirit needed to deter war be summoned without fully entering one?”

Reassurance force (slide 6): deploying European troops to Ukraine – even pre‑ceasefire – would do little for frontline defence, but the strategic necessity is psychological: it forces sheltered citizens to adopt a wartime mindset, forging genuine deterrence.

❷ The crashing waves · Middle East

🔥 Collapse of the Islamabad Memorandum slide 7

After seven consecutive nights of US strikes on Iranian military sites, deterrence in the Gulf evaporated. Iran suspended commitments to the US framework; Deputy Foreign Minister announced retaliation. The region pivots from fragile ceasefire to multi‑front offensive warfare.

💥 Multi‑front retaliation & chokepoints slides 8, 9, 10

Iran struck infrastructure in Kuwait, Bahrain, Jordan – desalination, power plants, airports, oil installations. slide 9

US enforces naval blockade in Strait of Hormuz (1/5 of global oil supply), while Iran uses missiles/drones against shipping. slide 10 Armed men seize a vessel off Yemen, destabilising the Red Sea chokepoint.

❸ The failure of the light · Keeper's Watch

🔦 The illusion of deterrence slides 12, 13

Two distinct storms reveal the same structural failure. Projecting power requires aligning three prisms: military capability (budgets, hardware), societal resilience (willingness to fight, rejection of propaganda), and political will (stationing troops, enforcing red lines). Without all three, the beam scatters – the storm overtakes the coast. slide 13

⏳ Maintaining the Keeper's Watch slide 14

Prepare in Peacetime: cognition and emotion must be forged before conflict. Europe must wake up to the hybrid war it is already in.
Protect the Mechanisms: modern conflict bypasses traditional fronts to dismantle the gears of society: water, power, data, transit.
Focus the Light: money alone does not deter. Only the combination of military hardware, political will, and deeply rooted societal resilience can keep the storm at bay.

📌 Summary · part 1
European nations are rearming not to directly intervene in Ukraine, but because a wider kinetic war with Russia seems increasingly plausible. However, surveys show that willingness to fight varies dramatically by geography – northern and eastern Europeans are far more prepared than southerners, partly due to media, politics, and proximity. The author, Nathalie Tocci, suggests that a European “reassurance force” deployed before a ceasefire could force citizens to confront reality, fostering the psychological resilience needed for deterrence – even if it puts troops at risk. slides 2, 6

📌 Summary · part 2
Simultaneously, the Iran–US conflict has escalated after a collapsed ceasefire. Iran has suspended its commitments under the Islamabad Memorandum, striking infrastructure in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan – targeting US bases, desalination plants, and oil facilities. The US enforces a naval blockade in the Strait of Hormuz while Iran threatens to stop all oil and gas exports from the region. Both sides exchange strikes on military and civilian sites, raising fears of a broader regional war, with Kuwait and other Gulf states caught in the crossfire. The Keeper’s Watch framework (slides 12–14) reveals that deterrence fails when military capability, societal resilience, and political will are not aligned. Preparing in peacetime and protecting critical mechanisms are essential.
